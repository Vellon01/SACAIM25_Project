CHAPTER 4

MODEL SELECTION, IMPLEMENTATION AND RESULTS

Introduction
In this chapter, we outline the detailed implementation of the machine learning and statistical models discussed in Chapter 3. The process encompasses training the models on the historical gold price data, evaluating their performance, and serving the forecasting system via an interactive web-based dashboard and API.

Model Selection and Implementation
As defined by the methodology, distinct model architectures were evaluated for their efficacy in long-term and immediate gold price forecasting:
1. ARIMA / SARIMA: Chosen as the primary statistical method due to its strong capability in forecasting temporal patterns. SARIMA was particularly configured to capture the monthly seasonality and general upward trajectory of long-term gold prices. 
2. Linear Regression: A standard regression model to establish a straightforward linear trend relationship of price movements over time.
3. Support Vector Machine (SVM): This advanced machine learning framework was employed to understand and predict immediate, non-linear month-to-month changes based on sliding window past values. Different kernels (Linear, Radial Basis Function, Polynomial) were applied to gauge the most effective representation of localized volatility.

Performance Evaluation
To rigorously assess the predictive capabilities of the models, standard statistical metrics including Root Mean Square Error (RMSE), Mean Absolute Error (MAE), and Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) were utilized. The performance evaluation highlights that while statistical baseline models maintain steady generalizations over multi-year horizons, SVM variants adapt with greater precision to shorter-term fluctuations.

CHAPTER 5

CONCLUSION

Summary
This research successfully explored and implemented the use of statistical and machine learning algorithms for the objective of long-term and immediate gold price forecasting. By constructing a 5-year outlook spanning from September 2025 to August 2030, this project serves as a cornerstone reference for analyzing future market growth vectors. The deployment of a unified predictive web interface and corresponding RESTful API further illustrates the capability of bridging advanced computational models directly into functional tools for stakeholders.

Key Findings
The comparative analysis revealed key operational dynamics between algorithms. The SVM models, especially those utilizing the Linear and Polynomial kernels, demonstrated significant capability in modeling the non-linear, immediate monthly adjustments observed in volatile modern markets. In contrast, the SARIMA model excelled at capturing broader macroeconomic trajectories, delivering reliable forward-looking projections that are stable enough for strategic multi-year planning. Thus, for extended five-year forecasting horizons, seasonal statistical formulations remain structurally favorable over purely computational non-linear mapping.

Limitations
Despite the extensive 25-year dataset and systematic methodology, external unpredictable market shocks—ranging from abrupt geopolitical shifts to unprecedented black swan economic crises—remain inherent challenges to pure quantitative models. The features analyzed do not account for external real-time text sentiment or newly announced global central bank policies that may abruptly override historical momentum.

Future Scope
Future iterations of this predictive framework hold significant potential for expansion. Further research can incorporate Deep Learning architectures, specifically architectures like CNN-LSTMs and Transformers, to simultaneously ingest chronological price action alongside high-velocity data points like macroeconomic indicators, US Dollar Index fluctuations, or news sentiment analytics. Additionally, integrating cloud-native model retraining pipelines could establish continuous learning infrastructures that evolve dynamically as the global marketplace continuously shifts.
